Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist


🔐 General Online Safety

Always verify the authenticity of emails, messages, and links before clicking on them, especially if they create urgency or ask for sensitive information.

Use strong, unique passwords for different accounts and avoid using easily guessable information like your name or date of birth.

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on important accounts such as email, banking apps, and social media platforms.

Avoid using public Wi-Fi networks for logging into sensitive accounts or making financial transactions.

Regularly update your apps, devices, and antivirus software to protect against security vulnerabilities.


💳 Financial & UPI Safety

Always verify the receiver’s name and details before sending money through UPI or any digital payment app.

Never approve unknown “collect requests” or payment requests, even if they appear to come from a known source.

Do not share OTPs, UPI PINs, or bank details with anyone under any circumstances, even if they claim to be from a bank.

Avoid scanning QR codes from unknown or untrusted sources, as they may lead to fraudulent transactions.


📱 Responsible Digital Behavior

Do not overshare personal information such as phone numbers, addresses, or identification details on public platforms.

Be cautious while downloading apps or files from unknown websites, as they may contain malware.

Always log out from shared or public devices after using your accounts.


🚨 Reporting Cybercrime

In case of any cyber fraud or suspicious activity, report immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).

You can also call the Cyber Crime Helpline Number: 1930 for quick assistance.
